## About Thekua

**What is Thekua?**  
Thekua is a traditional fried sweet from the Mithila region of northern Bihar and southern Nepal. It is made from stone-ground whole wheat flour (chakki atta), block jaggery or sugar, pure desi ghee, and dried grated coconut. It is pressed into a round or oval disc using a hand-carved wooden mould called a sancha, then fried slowly in desi ghee. No preservatives, no refined flour, no palm oil.

**Where does Thekua come from?**  
Thekua comes from Mithila — a cultural and linguistic region defined by the Maithili language, spanning northern Bihar and southern Nepal. It is not exclusively a "Bihari sweet" — it predates Bihar as an administrative state and is made on both sides of the India-Nepal border.

**Is Thekua only a Chhath Puja sweet?**  
No. Historically, Thekua was an everyday snack in Mithila homes — kept in a steel dabba on the kitchen shelf and eaten with chai, after school, during journeys. It became associated primarily with Chhath because festival occasions were harder to displace than everyday habits. The Thekua Company is bringing it back as an everyday snack.

**What does Thekua taste like?**  
Thekua has a deep, complex sweetness from jaggery — earthier and less flat than refined sugar. Desi ghee provides warmth and aroma. Whole wheat gives substance. Coconut adds crunch. The texture is firm and dense — not soft or melt-in-mouth. It holds its shape in chai without disintegrating. The jaggery variant is slightly more complex; the sugar variant is lighter.

**What does "Thekua" mean?**  
The word comes from "thokna" — meaning to hammer or press in Hindi/Maithili. It refers to the act of pressing the dough firmly onto the wooden sancha mould.

**What is a sancha mould?**  
The sancha is a hand-carved wooden mould used to press the Thekua dough into a round or oval disc with a floral or geometric pattern. Traditional sancha designs share their visual vocabulary with Madhubani painting. The mould also standardises thickness for even frying.

## Freshness & Storage

**Is every pack made fresh?**  
Yes. Every pack is prepared fresh after the order is placed — never before. No stock is kept.

**How long does Thekua stay fresh?**  
60–75 days in an airtight container at room temperature. Keep away from moisture and direct sunlight. Once opened, reseal after each use to retain crunch and aroma.

**Why does Thekua last so long without preservatives?**  
The combination of low moisture content, desi ghee (which acts as a natural oxygen barrier), and the frying process (which drives out residual moisture) prevents microbial growth. No artificial preservatives are needed or used.

**How should I store Thekua?**  
In an airtight container at room temperature. Away from moisture and direct sunlight. Reseal after every use.

## Ingredients & Nutrition

**What are the ingredients?**  
Chakki atta (stone-ground whole wheat flour), block jaggery or sugar, pure desi ghee, and dried grated coconut. No refined flour, no palm oil, no preservatives, no artificial flavouring, no additives.

**Are there any allergens?**  
Thekua contains wheat (gluten), dairy (ghee), and coconut. It does not contain nuts or artificial additives unless specified.

**Is Thekua healthy?**  
Thekua is made from four real, minimally processed ingredients. It is significantly better than biscuits and packaged snacks made from maida, refined sugar, and palm oil. It provides fibre (from whole wheat and coconut), iron, potassium, magnesium (from jaggery), B vitamins (from whole wheat), and fat-soluble vitamins and butyric acid (from ghee). However, it is a fried sweet with approximately 160–170 calories per piece. It is a better everyday snack — not a health supplement.

**How many calories are in one Thekua?**  
Approximately 160–170 calories per piece (~35g). Similar in calories to two Parle-G biscuits, but made from whole wheat, jaggery, and ghee instead of maida, refined sugar, and palm oil.

**Is Thekua suitable for diabetics?**  
Jaggery has a moderately lower glycemic index than refined sugar (~50–65 vs ~65–70), meaning a somewhat slower blood sugar rise. However, jaggery still raises blood sugar — it is a sweetener. Diabetics should consult their doctor before consuming. The company does not position Thekua as "diabetic-friendly."

**Is jaggery better than refined sugar?**  
Yes, in terms of nutrition. Jaggery retains trace minerals — iron (~11.4mg/100g), potassium (~1000mg/100g), magnesium (~70–90mg/100g) — that are stripped from white sugar during refining. However, jaggery is still a sweetener and still raises blood sugar. "Better than refined sugar" is not the same as "healthy."

**Is Thekua suitable for children?**  
Yes. The ingredients — whole wheat, jaggery/sugar, ghee — are the same used in Indian home kitchens for generations. No artificial additives or preservatives.

**Can I eat Thekua every day?**  
Yes — that's how it was historically eaten in Mithila. One to two pieces per day with chai is a reasonable serving, as part of a balanced diet.

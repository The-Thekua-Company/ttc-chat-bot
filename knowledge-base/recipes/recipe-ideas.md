## Beverage Pairings

- Classic combo: Thekua with hot masala chai — the earthy jaggery sweetness balances the spice.
- Jaggery variant pairs beautifully with filter coffee — the bitterness of coffee cuts through the sweetness.
- Sugar variant works well with a lightly spiced kahwa or plain black tea.
- Cold pairing: crushed Thekua sprinkled over a glass of chilled thandai during summer.

## Dessert & Recipe Ideas (Thekua as an ingredient)

- **Thekua Crumble**: Crush Thekua pieces over warm stewed fruit or custard as a quick crumble topping.
- **Thekua Kheer Topping**: Crumble Thekua over kheer or rice pudding for extra crunch and jaggery flavor.
- **Thekua Milkshake**: Blend a couple of pieces with milk, a banana, and a little extra jaggery for a quick dessert shake.
- **Thekua Trifle**: Layer crumbled Thekua with whipped cream and fruit for a festive trifle.
- **Thekua Ice Cream Sundae**: Crumbled Thekua as a crunchy topping over vanilla or malai ice cream.

## Festive Serving Ideas

- Arrange assorted Thekua pieces (jaggery + sugar variants) on a platter for Chhath Puja or Diwali guests.
- Pair with a small bowl of roasted makhana (fox nuts) for a festive snack tray.
- Great as an edible favor alongside a small card for weddings or housewarmings.

## Notes for the Assistant

- Thekua is fried in ghee, made from whole wheat flour, jaggery or sugar, and dried coconut — it is NOT gluten-free and contains dairy (ghee).
- Texture is firm and dense, not soft — factor this into recipe pairing suggestions (works well as a crunchy topping, holds shape when dunked).
- Shelf life is 60-75 days stored airtight — don't suggest recipes that require it to stay fresh/moist for extended periods without proper storage.

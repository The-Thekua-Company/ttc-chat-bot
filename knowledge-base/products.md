# Products — The Thekua Company

All products are handmade Thekua made fresh after each order by Mithila homemakers (Neetu Yadav and Geeta Singh). No pre-made stock is kept. Every batch uses the same three to four ingredients: chakki atta (stone-ground whole wheat flour), jaggery or sugar, pure desi ghee, and dried grated coconut.

**Two sweetener variants available across all sizes:**
- **Jaggery variant** — traditional, earthy, mineral-rich sweetness
- **Sugar variant** — slightly lighter in sweetness

## Common Product FAQs

**Is every pack made fresh?**  
Yes. Every pack is prepared fresh after the order is placed — never before. No stock is kept.

**Are all pack sizes made the same way?**  
Yes. Every pack — 250g, 500g, 1kg, 2kg — is prepared in the same small batches by the same homemakers. The quantity is larger but the process is identical. No batch production, no factory shortcuts.

**What is the shelf life?**  
60–75 days in an airtight container at room temperature. Keep away from moisture and direct sunlight. Once opened, reseal after each use to retain crunch and aroma.

**What does Thekua taste like?**  
Mild, natural sweetness — not the sharp sweetness of refined sugar sweets or packaged biscuits. The jaggery variant has an earthy, rounded sweetness. The sugar variant is slightly lighter. Both are balanced enough for daily snacking without feeling heavy. Firm texture that holds its shape in chai.

**Can I gift Thekua?**  
Yes. The 250g and 500g packs make thoughtful gifts for colleagues, family, and friends. For gift packaging or bulk gifting, contact support@thekuacompany.com.

**Is Thekua suitable for children?**  
Yes. Made with whole wheat, jaggery/sugar, and ghee — the same ingredients used in Indian home kitchens for generations. No preservatives, no refined flour, no palm oil.

**Is Thekua suitable for diabetics?**  
Jaggery is less processed than white sugar and retains trace minerals, but its glycemic index (~84) is actually higher than white sugar's (~65), meaning it can raise blood sugar levels quickly. Diabetics should consume in moderation and consult their doctor. The company does not position Thekua as "diabetic-friendly."

**Does jaggery have health benefits over sugar?**  
Jaggery retains trace minerals (iron ~11.4mg/100g, potassium ~1000mg/100g, magnesium ~70–90mg/100g) that are stripped from white sugar during refining. However, its glycemic index is higher than refined sugar's, so it raises blood sugar faster, not slower. "Nutritionally richer than refined sugar" is not the same as "lower-GI" or "healthy."

**Are there any allergens?**  
Thekua contains wheat (gluten) and coconut. It is made with ghee (dairy). No nuts unless specified. No artificial additives.

## 1. What Is Thekua? The Everyday Snack That Became a Festival Food
**URL:** https://thekuacompany.com/what-is-thekua/  
**Date:** June 29, 2026

### Key points:
- Thekua is a traditional fried sweet from Mithila (not just Bihar) made from chakki atta, block jaggery, desi ghee, and dried grated coconut.
- "Thekua" comes from "thokna" — to press or hammer — referring to pressing dough onto the sancha mould.
- Mithila is a civilisation predating Bihar as a state; the same recipe is made on both sides of the India-Nepal border.
- Four ingredients only: chakki atta, block jaggery, desi ghee, dried grated coconut. The coconut is the Mithila-specific detail most online recipes miss.
- The sancha mould standardises thickness for even frying and carries the same visual patterns as Madhubani painting — not a coincidence, the same cultural vocabulary.
- Thekua was historically everyday food, not festival food. Packaged snacks (Parle-G, Britannia etc.) entering tier-2 and tier-3 cities in the late 1980s and 1990s displaced it from daily occasions. Chhath Puja was the one occasion packaged snacks couldn't take from it — hence it became "Chhath food" in popular perception.
- The Thekua Company's mission is to rebuild the commercial infrastructure so Thekua can be in the kitchen every week.
- **PM Modi gifted Thekua to Richard Raší (Speaker of Slovakia's Parliament) in June 2026** — the first Indian PM to visit Slovakia since its independence.
- Available in 250g, 500g, 1kg, 2kg packs at thekuacompany.com.
- Shelf life: 60–75 days in an airtight container at room temperature (confirmed figure; this post's original 60–90 day claim has been corrected).

## 2. Thekua Nutrition: What's Actually in It (And What It Means for You)
**URL:** https://thekuacompany.com/thekua-nutrition/  
**Date:** June 17, 2026

### Key points:
- Honest nutritional analysis — no overclaiming. Thekua is a better snack than biscuits, not a superfood.
- **Per piece (20–24g):** ~80–110 kcal (jaggery variant ~81–97 kcal, sugar variant ~93–111 kcal), ~12–17g carbs, ~1.5–4.5g fat, ~1.5–2g protein, ~1.3–1.7g fibre.
- **Whole wheat flour:** ~12g fibre per 100g (vs maida at ~2–3g). Also provides B vitamins (thiamine, riboflavin, niacin), iron (~3.5–4mg/100g), magnesium (~138mg/100g). The fibre is why one Thekua with chai is more satiating than four biscuits.
- **Jaggery:** Iron ~11.4mg/100g (per IFCT 2017), potassium ~1000–1050mg/100g, magnesium ~70–90mg/100g. GI ~84, higher than refined sugar's ~65 — jaggery raises blood sugar faster, not slower, despite being less processed. Still a sweetener. Claims about "liver detoxification" or "blood purification" are not evidence-based.
- **Ghee:** Contains fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K; butyric acid (short-chain fatty acid important for gut health); small amounts of CLA. Rich in saturated fat (~62g per 100g). Better than refined palm oil but "better than palm oil" ≠ "heart-healthy."
- **Dried coconut:** Medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs), ~9g fibre/100g. Primarily there for texture — the crunch. Present at 50–60g per kg of atta.
- Recipe ratio: 500g jaggery per 1kg atta, 80–100g ghee in dough per kg atta, plus frying in ghee.
- Comparison table: Thekua ~80–110 kcal per piece (20–24g), with desi ghee + jaggery + no preservatives. vs Gulab Jamun ~150–175 kcal, Kaju Katli ~180–200 kcal, Parle-G (5 biscuits) ~175–185 kcal — note the Thekua figure is per single piece while the others are per comparable serving; per-gram, Thekua is not dramatically lower in calories, the advantage is ingredient quality, not calorie count.
- Thekua wins on ingredient quality, not calories.

## 5. Thekua Recipe: The Authentic Mithila Method
**URL:** https://thekuacompany.com/thekua-recipe-authentic/  
**Date:** May 12, 2026

### Key points (from context across the site):
- **Ingredients:** Chakki atta, block jaggery (dissolved into syrup first), desi ghee (in dough and for frying), dried grated coconut.
- **Dough method:** Ghee rubbed into flour first (coats proteins, produces short crisp texture). Jaggery syrup added gradually. Dough must be firm — stiff enough to hold shape, not sticky, not pliable like chapati dough. Soft dough absorbs ghee during frying instead of being fried in it.
- **Resting:** Dough rested before pressing.
- **Pressing:** Dough pressed into sancha mould to create floral/geometric pattern and standardise thickness.
- **Frying:** Low flame, 12–15 minutes in desi ghee. Temperature must stay 160–170°C. High heat burns outside before centre sets. Jaggery caramelises faster than refined sugar — this is why low, slow frying is essential.
- **Result:** Deep golden brown, firm, crisp exterior.

## 12. From Field to Festival: The Complete Supply Chain of Authentic Thekua
**URL:** https://thekuacompany.com/from-field-to-festival-the-complete-supply-chain-of-authentic-thekua/  
**Date:** March 15, 2026

### Key points:
- Full supply chain from wheat farming → stone milling → jaggery sourcing → ghee sourcing → dough preparation → pressing → frying → packaging → delivery.
- **Wheat:** Chakki atta (stone-ground) is used, not roller-milled. Stone grinding keeps natural oils and nutrients intact, gives roasted aroma and wholesome mouthfeel. Roller mills are faster but strip bran and germ.
- **Jaggery:** Sourced from Muzaffarpur and Champaran producers — known for balanced flavour and clean, natural profile without artificial colours or sweeteners (common in adulterated jaggery).
- **Ghee:** Sourced from dairy cooperatives and traditional ghee-makers. No vanaspati or blended fats.
- **Frying:** Must maintain 160–170°C. Traditional artisans use wood or coal fires, gauging heat by colour and sound. The Thekua Company balances traditional intuition with modern precision using temperature controls.
- **Chhath season:** When demand soars, many producers switch to machine moulds. The Thekua Company does not — every piece is hand-shaped year-round.
- **Packaging:** Airtight jars, packed after cooling, to retain freshness and crunch during transit.
- **Sustainability:** Exploring eco-friendly packaging, responsible oil disposal, and renewable energy in production.
